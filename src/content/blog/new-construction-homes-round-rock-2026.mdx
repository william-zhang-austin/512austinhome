## Top New Construction Communities in Round Rock

### Homestead Village -- Best Value in Round Rock ISD

Homestead Village is where the math works for buyers who want Round Rock ISD without stretching past $400K. Meritage Homes builds here with their standard energy-efficiency package -- ENERGY STAR appliances, spray-foam insulation, and a HERS rating that typically runs 30-40% more efficient than code minimum. That translates to $50-$100 per month in lower utility bills compared to a resale home of similar size.

The location is east Round Rock, roughly 10 minutes from Dell headquarters. For Dell employees, this is the most direct commute-to-price ratio in the city. The floor plans are compact -- 1,400 sqft on the low end is a functional 3-bedroom but not spacious. This is a starter home or a downsizer, not a move-up.

The watch-out is active construction. Homestead Village is still building out, which means construction traffic, early-morning starts, and unfinished streetscapes for the next 1-2 years. That is normal for new communities, but set your expectations accordingly.

### Siena -- Best All-Around Community

Siena is Taylor Morrison's mid-range Round Rock offering, and it is the community I send most buyers to first when they are shopping in the $420K-$600K range. Taylor Morrison's finishes are a step above typical production builders -- better cabinetry, more thoughtful floor plan flow, and a design center with options that do not all carry steep upcharges.

The community has established amenities, which matters more than most buyers realize. A pool that exists today is worth more than a pool that is "coming soon" on the site plan. Siena has that advantage over newer communities still in early phases.

The floor plan range -- 1,800 to 3,200 sqft across 3-5 bedrooms -- covers first-time buyers through growing families. The trade-off is commute time: 25-35 minutes to downtown Austin depending on traffic and time of day. For buyers working at Dell, Apple, or Samsung, the commute is shorter (10-20 minutes), which is the real calculus.

### The Highlands at Mayfield Ranch -- Best for Move-Up Buyers

The Highlands at Mayfield Ranch is Highland Homes building inside an established master-planned community, and that combination matters. Mayfield Ranch already has a community center, pool, parks, and trail system in place. You are not buying into a promise -- the infrastructure is built and maintained.

Highland Homes has a strong reputation among Central Texas builders for above-average construction quality. Their standard specs include upgraded framing, better insulation packages, and finishes that compete with semi-custom builders at a production-home price point. The floor plans run 2,200 to 3,500 sqft, with most buyers landing in the 2,800-3,200 range.

At $500K-$650K, this is the premium tier for Round Rock new construction. The proximity to both Dell and Apple (via Toll 45) makes it a strong fit for dual-income tech households. The watch-out is I-35 commute congestion -- if your route to work runs through the I-35 / Toll 45 interchange during peak hours, budget an extra 10-15 minutes over what Google Maps shows at noon.

All three Round Rock new construction communities are zoned to **Round Rock ISD**, one of the highest-rated public school districts in the Austin metro. This is a significant differentiator from neighboring cities. Round Rock ISD consistently ranks among the top districts in Texas for college readiness, standardized test scores, and extracurricular programs.

Key schools for new construction zoning include multiple well-regarded elementary and middle school options, with Round Rock, Stony Point, Cedar Ridge, and Westwood as the primary high schools. Specific zoning depends on which community you choose -- always verify current attendance boundaries before making an offer.

Round Rock ISD is the reason many buyers pay a premium to be in Round Rock versus Pflugerville or Hutto, where comparable square footage can cost $30K-$80K less. If school quality is your primary driver, Round Rock delivers.

## What to Watch Out for in Round Rock New Construction

**The price floor is higher than neighboring cities.** Pflugerville and Hutto both offer new construction starting in the high $200s to low $300s. Round Rock's entry point at $310K (Homestead Village) is competitive, but the next tier jumps to $420K. If your budget is $300K-$400K and you need more than a compact floor plan, look at Pflugerville's deeper inventory in that range.

**I-35 congestion is real.** Round Rock straddles I-35, and peak-hour commutes to downtown Austin can run 40-50 minutes even though the distance is only 20 miles. Toll 45 helps if your destination is northwest Austin (Apple) or east (Samsung), but downtown-bound commuters sit in I-35 traffic. Factor toll costs into your monthly budget -- Toll 45 can run $150-$250 per month for daily commuters.

**Builder base finishes vary.** Meritage's energy-efficiency package at Homestead Village is strong, but the interior finishes at their base level are modest. Highland Homes at Mayfield Ranch includes upgrades that other builders charge extra for. Taylor Morrison falls in between. Model homes are always upgraded beyond standard -- ask specifically what is included at the base price. I wrote a [detailed breakdown of the five costliest new construction mistakes](/blog/new-construction-mistakes-austin/) that covers this and other expensive surprises.

**Active construction zones.** Homestead Village is still in active build-out. The Highlands at Mayfield Ranch, being part of an established master plan, has less construction disruption in the surrounding area. If living adjacent to active construction bothers you, prioritize communities in later build phases.

## Commute Times from Round Rock

Round Rock's position along I-35 and Toll 45 gives you direct access to the major Austin-area employers:

- **Dell (Round Rock)** -- 5-10 minutes. Dell headquarters is in Round Rock. This is the shortest commute of any Austin suburb to a major employer.
- **Apple (northwest Austin)** -- 15-20 minutes via Toll 45 to Toll 183A.
- **Samsung Taylor** -- 20-25 minutes via Toll 130.
- **Emerson (Round Rock)** -- 10-15 minutes. Another local employer that keeps commutes short.
- **Downtown Austin** -- 25-40 minutes via I-35. The range is wide because I-35 congestion varies dramatically by time of day.
- **Tesla Gigafactory** -- 30-40 minutes southeast via Toll 130 to SH 45.

The calculus for most Round Rock buyers is simple: if you work at Dell, Apple, or Samsung, Round Rock is one of the best commute-to-value suburbs in the metro. If you work downtown, the I-35 corridor is the bottleneck.
