## Top New Construction Communities in Hutto

### Star Ranch -- Best Overall Value

Star Ranch is Hutto's flagship master-planned community, built around an 18-hole championship golf course across 1,000 acres. Meritage Homes and Lennar both build here, which means you can compare two national builders' floor plans, pricing, and incentives without driving to a second community. That is rare and gives you negotiating leverage.

Pricing starts at $280K for entry-level plans around 1,400 sqft and runs to $430K for 2,800 sqft homes with 5 bedrooms. Lennar's "Everything's Included" model keeps the base price closer to the final price, while Meritage's energy-efficiency packages lower your monthly utility costs. The resort-style pool, community parks, and golf course access set Star Ranch apart from other Hutto communities on amenities.

The trade-offs: Star Ranch is in a MUD, so calculate your Year 2 tax bill carefully. Hutto ISD serves the community -- adequate but not top-ranked. And you are 35+ minutes from downtown Austin during peak hours.

### Emory Crossing -- Best Finishes

Emory Crossing is Taylor Morrison's entry into Hutto, and it brings the design center experience that Taylor Morrison is known for in the Austin market. The community is newer than Star Ranch, which means better lot selection and early-phase pricing. Homes range from $350K to $550K across 1,800 to 3,200 sqft, with 3-5 bedroom layouts.

Taylor Morrison's design center finishes are a step above typical production builders. If countertops, flooring, and cabinet quality matter to you, this is where to start in Hutto. The community is still in active construction, which means dust and construction traffic -- but it also means the builder is motivated to sell and incentives are strong.

David Weekley Homes also has a presence in the Emory Crossing area with townhome-style product starting from $259,990, offering a lower entry point for buyers who prioritize the Taylor Morrison neighborhood but need a more accessible price.

### Legends Village -- Best for Comparison Shopping

Legends Village brings multiple builders into one community -- KB Home, Buffington Homes, and others have built here. That means you can walk three or four model homes in one afternoon and compare finishes, floor plans, and pricing side by side. Pricing runs from $300K to $480K across 1,500 to 2,800 sqft.

Buffington Homes built Energy Star Certified homes here with 3-sided stone or ACME brick exteriors and 3-car garages -- details that matter for resale value. KB Home offers their build-to-order model, which gives you more control over finishes than a spec home.

The variety of builders also means the community has a less uniform look than a single-builder neighborhood. Some buyers see that as character; others prefer the consistency of a master-planned community like Star Ranch.

### Riverwalk -- Best for Growing Families

Riverwalk pairs Ashton Woods and Meritage Homes across 1,600 to 3,000 sqft floor plans priced from $320K to $500K. Ashton Woods has strong design credibility in the Austin market -- their Rolling Glen community in Hutto (575 lots, 11 floor plans) has been well received -- and that design sensibility carries into Riverwalk.

Meritage brings their energy-efficiency package, which can save $50-$100/month on utilities depending on the plan. For a family buying in the $400K range, that adds up. Community amenities round out the offering for families with school-age children.

Budget for Ashton Woods design center upgrades on countertops and flooring -- their base-level finishes are modest, and the model homes are always upgraded.

## School Districts Serving Hutto New Construction

All Hutto new construction communities are zoned to **Hutto ISD**. The district has an 84.7% graduation rate and is investing in new facilities to keep pace with Hutto's growth. Hutto ISD is not top-ranked like Round Rock ISD, Leander ISD, or Eanes ISD, but it serves the community adequately and is improving.

For buyers whose top priority is school rankings, Round Rock ISD communities in Round Rock are 15-20 minutes southwest. The trade-off is price: comparable new construction in Round Rock ISD often costs $50K-$100K more than the same square footage in Hutto. Many families find the price savings fund private tutoring, extracurriculars, or a larger home -- and come out ahead financially.

## What to Watch Out for in Hutto New Construction

**MUD taxes are the norm.** Most Hutto new construction communities sit in Municipal Utility Districts. The extra 0.5-1.0% on your tax rate does not sound like much until you calculate it on a $400K home -- that is $2,000-$4,000 per year on top of your base property taxes. Year 2 is where the biggest shock happens because your property gets reassessed at full market value after construction. Always ask for the MUD disclosure and calculate your Year 2 tax estimate.

**The commute to downtown Austin is real.** Hutto is 35-50 minutes from downtown, and there is no toll road shortcut for that route. If your job is downtown, this commute will define your quality of life. Hutto works best for buyers commuting to north Austin, Round Rock, or the Samsung Taylor campus in Taylor.

**Hutto ISD is not a selling point.** The district is adequate and improving, but it does not carry the same resale premium as Round Rock ISD or Leander ISD. If you plan to sell in 5-7 years, factor in that school district perception affects your buyer pool.

**Active construction is everywhere.** Hutto is growing fast, and most communities are mid-build-out. Expect construction traffic, dust, and noise for 1-3 years. If that bothers you, look for communities that are 60-70% complete.

Hutto's location works best for north Austin and Taylor corridor employers. Here are the realistic drive times:

- **Samsung Taylor** -- 15-20 minutes east via US 79. This is Hutto's closest major employer and the primary reason for buyer demand.
- **Dell (Round Rock)** -- 20 minutes southwest via Toll 130 to University Blvd.
- **Apple (northwest Austin)** -- 25-35 minutes via Toll 130 to Toll 45 to Toll 183A.
- **Tesla Gigafactory** -- 30-35 minutes southeast via Toll 130 to SH 45.
- **Downtown Austin** -- 35-50 minutes via I-35. Peak-hour traffic on I-35 pushes this toward the high end consistently.

Toll 130 is the route that makes Hutto work. Without it, every commute adds 15-20 minutes on surface roads. Budget $100-$150/month for tolls if you are commuting daily.
