## Top New Construction Communities in Pflugerville

### Meadowlark Preserve -- Best Entry Point

Meadowlark Preserve is Lennar's 98-acre master-planned community in east Pflugerville and the most affordable new construction option in the city. The Lonestar Collection starts at $314,990, with the Claremont Collection stepping up from there. At buildout, the community will have 375 homes plus 25 acres of parks and open space.

Lennar's "Everything's Included" pricing model means fewer surprises at the design center -- the base price is closer to the final price than with most builders. The Amazon fulfillment center (1,000+ jobs) is three miles away, which adds a hiring anchor beyond the typical tech corridor employers.

The trade-off is size. Homes under 1,500 sqft are compact starter layouts, not family-sized. And Lennar's included-everything model means fewer customization options if you want to put your own stamp on finishes.

### Lisso -- Best for Tech Commuters

Lisso is the community I send most tech workers to first. Taylor Morrison builds across 40s, 50s, and 60s lot sizes, with current pricing from $430,990. The Lavaca floor plan (5 bed, downstairs master) is the flagship -- a downstairs master at this price point is rare in the Austin metro. The Colorado at 2,172 sqft is the value play.

Central Pflugerville location means you are five minutes from Costco and H-E-B and 15 minutes to Apple, Samsung, Dell, and Tesla via Toll 130 and Toll 45. The amenity package (pool, pickleball courts, soccer field, playground) runs under $50/month HOA, which is below average for the quality. Taylor Morrison's design center finishes are a step above typical production builders.

The community is in a MUD, so factor that into your tax calculation. And lot orientation matters here -- west-facing homes take heavy afternoon sun during Austin summers.

### The Grove at Blackhawk -- Best Amenities

The Grove is the newest phase of Blackhawk, Pflugerville's flagship master-planned community. If you want an 8-lane Olympic pool, a lagoon pool, a clubhouse, and miles of hiking and biking trails, this is the only way to get them in a new build. Chesmar Homes offers 16 floor plans from 1,931 to 3,033 sqft, with pricing from $469K to $749K.

Chesmar is a Texas-focused builder with a strong reputation for above-average finishes. The community sits minutes from Tesla Gigafactory, Dell, and Samsung Taylor. At the upper end, homes approach $750K -- this is mid-market to premium, not entry-level.

### The Trails at Carmel -- Widest Price Range

Carmel covers more ground than any other Pflugerville community -- Ashton Woods builds from $350K to $705K in one neighborhood, with 40s, 50s, 60s, and 70s home collections. The community is still in its early phase, which means better lot selection and more pricing flexibility than established sections. Weiss High School zoning is a draw for families.

Ashton Woods has strong design credibility, but their base-level finishes are modest. Budget for design center upgrades on countertops and flooring. Amenities are still "coming soon" -- ask for a construction timeline before assuming the pool will be ready by the time you move in.

## School Districts Serving Pflugerville New Construction

All Pflugerville new construction communities are zoned to **Pflugerville ISD**. The district is solid and improving, with multiple elementary and high school options. Weiss High School and Hendrickson High School are the two most common assignments for new construction communities.

Pflugerville ISD is not top-ranked like Leander ISD or Eanes ISD. For buyers whose top priority is school rankings, Leander ISD communities are 10-15 minutes north in Leander and Cedar Park. For buyers who prioritize location, value, and tech corridor proximity, Pflugerville ISD is a strong choice -- especially when the price difference between Pflugerville and Leander ISD communities can be $50K-$100K+ for comparable square footage.

## What to Watch Out for in Pflugerville New Construction

**MUD taxes are everywhere.** Most Pflugerville new construction communities sit in Municipal Utility Districts. The extra 0.5-1.0% on your tax rate does not sound like much until you calculate it on a $450K home -- that is $2,250-$4,500 per year on top of your base property taxes. Always ask for the MUD disclosure and calculate your Year 2 tax estimate. MUD rates decrease over time as the debt gets paid off, but Year 1 to Year 2 is where the biggest shock happens because your property gets reassessed at full market value after construction. I wrote a [detailed breakdown of the five costliest new construction mistakes](/blog/new-construction-mistakes-austin/) that covers this and four other expensive surprises.

**Builder base finishes vary dramatically.** Lennar's "Everything's Included" at Meadowlark Preserve looks different from Chesmar's standard package at The Grove at Blackhawk. Model homes are always upgraded -- ask specifically what is standard and what is an upgrade before running your budget. For upgrade strategy, see my guide on [which upgrades actually deliver ROI](/blog/new-construction-upgrades-highest-roi/).

**The commute depends on which Pflugerville.** Central Pflugerville communities like Lisso are 15 minutes from Apple and Samsung. East Pflugerville communities like Meadowlark Preserve and Trails at Carmel add 5-10 minutes. That difference compounds over a year of commuting.

**Active construction is the norm.** Most of these communities are mid-build-out, which means construction traffic, dust, and early-morning noise for 1-3 years. If you are buying in a grand-opening phase community like Trails at Wildhorse, expect this to be part of your daily life until the community reaches 60-70% completion.

## Commute Times from Pflugerville

Pflugerville is one of the best-positioned Austin suburbs for tech employers. Toll 130 and Toll 45 are the arteries that make it work:

- **Samsung Taylor** -- 15-20 minutes via Toll 130. This is the fastest route to the semiconductor corridor.
- **Tesla Gigafactory** -- 20-25 minutes southeast via Toll 130 to SH 45.
- **Dell (Round Rock)** -- 15 minutes north on Toll 45.
- **Apple (northwest Austin)** -- 20-25 minutes via Toll 45 to Toll 183A.
- **Amazon Fulfillment Center** -- 5-10 minutes from east Pflugerville communities.
- **Downtown Austin** -- 20-30 minutes via I-35, depending on traffic and which part of Pflugerville you live in.

The toll road access is the reason Pflugerville works for tech commuters. Without it, the commute times would be 30-40% longer on surface roads.
